Meta Commentary and General Guidelines

General Guidelines

1. All entries should be written in the past tense. This includes in-game text.

2. Entries should be organized from broader to more specific subjects, in the following order:

  1. World
  2. Civilizations and Groups
  3. Sites and Events
  4. Historical Figures

2.1. A historical figure refers to any creature, sapient or not. An entry on a historical figure should contain, if possible: a summary, a description, a chronology, a see also section and a kill list, in order. Deities should be documented separately in deities.html, following a different set of rules and guidelines.

2.1.1. A summary should consist of a brief paragraph, including as much information as possible.
It should start with the name of the subject, followed by its gender, species, year and place of birth. For deceased subjects, include the year of death in the format yyy (d. yyy). Next, their parentage. If no information regarding their ancestry exists, they should be listed as being of unknown parentage.
Next, they should be described for what they are known for. Information regarding their past titles, offices, professions, etc. should be included afterwards. After that, their spouses and children should be mentioned.
Information regarding their deities and affiliation with religious groups comes last.

2.1.2. A genealogy should go through all generations of creatures until the very first one, following agnatic succession. All children common to the relevant spouse (in case of multiple ones) should be included. The spouses themselves should be omitted.

2.1.3. A description should contain the in-game creature menu information from the following tabs, in order:

  1. "Health > Description" (except the racial description)
  2. "Personality > Traits";
  3. "Personality > Values";
  4. "Personality > Preferences".

The highlighted in-game text should also be highlighted in the entries using the classes: .strength, .weakness, .quirk, .values, .trauma and .dream. The main body of the "Personality > Values" tab should not be highlighted in gray.

2.1.4. A chronology should document key events pertaining to the life of the subject, ordered by their occurrence in time. Dating of events should be as accurate as possible, including the day, month and year, whenever possible. In the absence of a date, an estimate should be given. Months should be referred by their dwarven calendar equivalent. See calendar for reference.

2.1.5. The see also section should include the information under the Related Historical Figures and Related Entities sections found in legends mode. Groups listed under the see also section should always be written in Dwarven followed by English (see rule 3.2).

2.1.6. A kill list should include the information under the Notable Kills and Other Kills sections found in legends mode.

3. The first occurrence of a proper noun in an entry should be written in Dwarven, followed by the English translation in quotation marks and separated by commas. Subsequent occurrences should be written in Dwarven only, with the English translation included in the HTML title attribute.

3.1. Creatures and deities are exempt from this rule. Only entries where the English translation is required is for deities.html and index-dwarf.html.

3.2. Group names under the "Related Groups" section of creature entries are also exempt from this rule. They should always be written in Dwarven, followed by the English translation.

4. Title case should be used for proper nouns.

4.1. Always capitalize: first and last word of a title and the first word after a colon, em dash or end punctuation, even if it would otherwise be lowercase; nouns, verbs, adverbs, subordinating conjunctions, adjectives and pronouns; long prepositions, that is, all prepositions with four or more letters.

4.2. Do not capitalize: articles; coordinating conjunctions; short prepositions, that is, all prepositions with three letters or less; "to" when used to form an infinitive.
