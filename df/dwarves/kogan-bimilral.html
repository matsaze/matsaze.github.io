Kogan Bimilral

Summary

Kogan Bimilral was a male dwarf born in 169. He was of unknown parentage. He was the King of Uzol Etost, "The Oil of Containing", and the former Baron of Konoslektad, "Brasslured", as well as the former general and diplomat of Uzol Etost. He was married to Zas Melbilthad. He was a worshipper of Tinöth Nethdèg Tinöththir Nulral and member of Maskirzocol, "The Equivalent Coven", an organized religion centered around the worship of Tinöth Nethdèg Tinöththir Nulral.

Description

He was slow to heal.

His very long beard was arranged in double braids. His sideburns were clean-shaven. His very long mustache was arranged in double braids. His long hair was tied in a pony tail. He had a scratchy voice. His somewhat narrow, rust eyes were close-set. His hair was buff with flecks of gray. His skin was brown.

He had good creativity, but he had a shortage of patience and a very bad sense of empathy.

He sought out exciting and adventurous situations. He could sometimes act without deliberation. He was somewhat quarrelsome, and he was bothered by this since he valued friendship. He preferred to present himself modestly. He sometimes acted with little determination and confidence. He often felt envious of others. He generally acted with a narrow focus on the current activity. He had an active sense of humor. He tended to be a bit stubborn in changing his mind about things.

He kept his voice very quiet when he was nervous. He winked during conversations.

He needed alcohol to get through the working day.

Like others in his culture, he held craftsdwarfship to be of the highest ideals and celebrated talented artisans and their masterworks, had a great deal of respect for the law, greatly prized loyalty, valued family greatly, saw friendship as one of the finer things in life, believed that honesty was a high ideal, greatly respected artists and their works, really respected those that took the time to master a skill, deeply respected those that worked hard at their labors, respected fair-dealing and fair-play, valued cooperation, found merrymaking and partying worthwhile activities, valued martial prowess, valued leisure time, respected commerce, valued knowledge and found nature somewhat disturbing. He personally saw concealment of emotions as a betrayal and tried his best never to associate with such secretive fools (due to being caught in a snow storm in 256). He personally thought romance was very important in life, respected power and found maintaining decorum a silly, fumbling waste of time. He dreamt of falling in love.

He liked porcelain, trifle pewter, green tourmaline, shields, goblets, the words of The Sweetness of Scholars, the sound of The Plays of Aquamarine and the sight of The Sparkle of Cherishing. When possible, he preferred to consume giant rattlesnake, cave lobster and passion fruit wine. He absolutely detested worms.

Chronology

In 250, Kogan Bimilral became a tavern keeper at Kugasluspen, "The Unholy Plum" in Ngebosmunstu, "Worklie".

In the early spring of 250, he quickly ascended through the ranks of the Kingdom of Uzol Etost, going through the offices of diplomat and general, before becoming the Baron of Konoslektad. He settled in Konoslektad and later became King of Uzol Etost.

In the late summer of 250, he married Zas Melbilthad.

In the early spring of 250, he sent forth an expedition of seven dwarves to The Admired Point to settle an outpost. On the 15th of Granite, 250, Amudtun, "Thunderdoors", was founded.

19th of Limestone, 252, he elevated Amudtun to a barony, bestowing the title of Baron of Amudtun to Thîkut Egdothkeskal.

16th of Limestone, 253, he elevated Amudtun to a county, bestowing the title of Count of Amudtun to Thîkut Egdothkeskal.

16th of Limestone, 253, he elevated Amudtun to a duchy, bestowing the title of Duke of Amudtun to Thîkut Egdothkeskal.

16th of Slate, 256, he settled in Amudtun, accompanied by Queen Consort Zas Melbilthad and his entourage, transferring the capital of the Kingdom of Uzol Etost.

In the late winter of 256, he became a member of Rûlîton, "The Submerged Hall", a fishery worker's guild.
