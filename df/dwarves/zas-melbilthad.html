Zas Melbilthad

Summary

Zas Melbilthad was a female dwarf born in 130, in the hillocks of Shelretshorast, "Trancewires", in the Kingdom of Tangakast, "The Spotted Sabre". She was the eldest daughter of Nil Dastotrâluk. She was the Queen consort of Uzol Etost, "The Oil of Containing", during the reign of King Kogan Bimilral. She was an eminent mathematician and logician, former student of Inod Nunoksarvesh. She also dabbled in the topics of philosophy, history, astronomy, natural history, chemistry, geography, medicine and engineering. She was a worshipper of Oth Shetbêthnëlas, Stettad, Sostet Belar Bocash, Rîsen Zim, Os Kiksethal, Bardum and member of Isul Nïng, "The Faith of Burning", a dwarven religion centered around the worship of Bardum.

Description

She was incredibly tough and quick to heal.

Her very long hair was tied in a pony tail. Her nose was incredibly upturned. She had a grating, raspy voice. Her heliotrope eyes were protruding. Her small-lobed, narrow ears were very short. Her eyebrows were extremely sparse. Her head was somewhat tall. Her hair was gray with a touch of ochre. Her skin was dark peach.

She had a lot of willpower and the ability to focus, but she had an iffy sense for music and poor analytical abilities.

She was swayed by emotional appeals. She was given to flights of fancy to the point of distraction. She was greatly pleased by her own looks and accomplishments. She didn't mind a little tumult and discord in day-to-day living. She was grateful when others helped her out and tried to return favors. She liked to take it easy. She was somewhat fearful in the face of imminent danger. She often felt discouraged. She generally acted with a narrow focus on the current activity. She tried to keep her things orderly. She was not particularly interested in what others thought of her.

Her hands were animated when she spoke.

She needed alcohol to get through the working day.

Like others in her culture, she held craftsdwarfship to be of the highest ideals and celebrated talented artisans and their masterworks, had a great deal of respect for the law, saw friendship as one of the finer things in life, believed that honesty was a high ideal, really respected those that took their time to master a skill, deeply respected those that worked hard at their labors, found merrymaking and partying worthwhile activities, valued martial prowess, valued leisure time, respected commerce, valued cooperation, valued knowledge, respected fair-dealing and fair-play and found nature somewhat disturbing. She personally valued artwork, didn't particularly value loyalty, didn't care about nature one way or another (less extreme after being caught in a snow storm in 256) and did not care about family one way or the other. She dreamt of crafting a masterwork someday.

She liked pitchblende, copper, sapphire, giant mole leather, the words of The Festive Wisdom, the sound of The Earth of Gravel and the sight of The Sparkle of Cherishing. When possible, she preferred to consume passion fruit wine. She absolutely detested brown recluse spiders.

Chronology

In 130, Zas Melbilthad was born to Mistêm Fikodsefol and Nil Dastotrâluk.

In 148, she became a fisherdwarf in Shelretshorast.

In 151, she gave up being a fisherdwarf to become a mathematician in Shelretshorast.

In the late spring of 154, she moved to study in Aranmebzuth, "Sculptedoars", in order to pursue scholarship. Around that time, she started working at Bërûl Turel, "The Bastion of Learning", in Aranmebzuth.

In 155, she married Nomal Rakustkacoth. Later that year, she stopped working at Bërûl Turel and settled in Efivanarena, "Mobfurs".

In the midwinter of 159, she moved to study in Okilnomal, "Teststaff", in order to pursue scholarship. Around that time, she started working at Koshosh Amal, "The Library of Teachers", in Okilnomal.

In the midwinter of 160, Mastering the Algorithm was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

Mastering the Algorithm was a legendary faint yellow diamond-bound codex. The written portion consisted of a 30 page manual entitled Mastering the Algorithm, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned an algorithm for dividing one number into another, possibly yielding a remainder. The writing had a touch of melancholy yet it contained some asides related to the author's preferences. Overall, the prose was passable.

In 169, she divorced Nomal Rakustkacoth.

In the late autumn of 171, The World of Division was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

The World of Division was a legendary giant cockatiel parchment scroll. The rollers were made from moonstone. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled The World of Division, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned an algorithm for dividing one number into another, possibly yielding a remainder. The writing was quite self-indulgent and it had a touch of melancholy. Overall, the prose was not awful, but not very good either.

In the midspring of 181, The Algorithm: The Definitive Guide was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

The Algorithm: The Definitive Guide was a legendary wood opal-bound codex. The written portion consisted of a 56 page manual entitled The Algorithm: The Definitive Guide, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned an algorithm for dividing one number into another, possibly yielding a remainder. The writing was thoroughly organized yet it was depressing. Overall, the prose was passable.

In the early winter of 198, she was denied an apprenticeship under Adil Zatudil in Okilnomal, as the latter preferred to work alone.

In the late autumn of 199, For the Love of Division was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

For the Love of Division was a legendary conglomerate-bound codex. The written portion consisted of a 97 page manual entitled For the Love of Division, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned an algorithm for dividing one number into another, possibly yielding a remainder. The writing had a touch of melancholy and it was somewhat self-indulgent. Overall, the prose was amateurish at best.

In 201, she married Ber Vathezrimtar.

In the late spring of 206, she began an apprenticeship under Inod Nunoksarvesh.

In 209, she divorced Ber Vathezrimtar.

In the midautumn of 209, Division: Fact or Fiction? was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

Division: Fact or Fiction? was a legendary melanite-bound codex. The written portion consisted of a 32 page manual entitled Division: Fact or Fiction?, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned an algorithm for dividing one number into another, possibly yielding a remainder. The writing overflowed with sadness yet it was thoroughly organized. Overall, the prose was passable.

In the late autumn of 218, Absence: Principles and Practice was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

Division: Fact or Fiction? was a legendary white jade-bound codex. The written portion consisted of a 27 page manual entitled Absence: Principles and Practice, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned a symbol for nothingness. The writing contained some asides related to the author's preferences. Overall, the prose was passable.

In 224, she married Litast Uzolthad. Later that year, she stopped working at Koshosh Amal in Okilnomal to settle in Cíviraefeye, "Russetboats".

In the midspring of 224, she moved to study in Okilnomal in order to be with her master, Inod Nunoksarvesh. She started working at Koshosh Amal again.

In 225, she divorced Litast Uzolthad.

In 232, she married Adil Udarsarvesh.

In the midautumn of 237, Unknown Balancing and Completion was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

Unknown Balancing and Completion was a legendary bleak troll parchment scroll. The rollers were made from white jade. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled Unknown Balancing and Completion, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned the techniques of balancing and completion for solving equations. The writing was clearly organized and it artfully indulged the author's fancies. Overall, the prose was great.

In 247, she was widowed after her spouse, Adil Udarsarvesh, died of old age.

In the midautumn of 249, It All Begins With the Formula was authored by her in Okilnomal. It was stored in Koshosh Amal.

It All Begins With the Formula was a legendary forgotten beast parchment scroll. The rollers were made from alunite. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled It All Begins With the Formula, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned simple formulas for certain arbitrarily large sums. The writing was a bit stiff and it had a touch of melancholy. Overall, the prose was not awful, but was not very good either.

In the late summer of 250, she settled in Konoslektad, "Brasslured", after marrying King Kogan Bimilral.

16th of Slate, 256, Queen Consort Zas Melbilthad arrived in Amudtun, accompanied by King Kogan Bimilral.

In the midspring of 256, she gave up being a mathematician to become a sage in Amudtun. She started working at Mishthemrovol, "The Scholarly Sanctuary".

In the late winter of 256, On the Wind was authored by her in Amudtun. It was stored in Mishthemrovol.

On the Wind was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was a masterful pig tail scroll created by Cog Zatamerush. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from galena. The rollers were made from masterfully worked iron by Cog Zatamerush. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled On the Wind, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned the forces that govern wind patterns. The writing was depressing yet it artfully indulged the author's fancies. Overall, the prose was passable.

3rd of Granite, 257, she became the master of Kol Lanlarmeng.

In the early spring of 257, The Lens and the Universe and The Interpretation of Surveying were authored by her in Amudtun. They were stored in Mishthemrovol.

The Lens and the Universe was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was an exceptional pig tail scroll. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from shale. The rollers were made from well-crafted iron. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled The Lens and the Universe, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned the construction and use of the crystal lens. The writing was organized. Overall, the prose was great.

The Interpretation of Surveying was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was a superior quality pig tail scroll. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from bismuthinite. The rollers were made from superior quality iron. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled The Interpretation of Surveying, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned process of surveying land. The writing was clearly organized yet it was depressing. Overall, the prose was great.

In the midsummer of 257, The World of the Solution and Factual Your Own Story were authored by her in Amudtun. They were stored in Mishthemrovol.

The World of the Solution was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was an exceptional pig tail scroll. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from gabbro. The rollers were made from masterfully worked iron by Cog Zatamerush. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled The World of the Solution, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned methods for solving systems of equations. The writing was depressing. Overall, was not awful, but not very good either.

Factual Your Own Story was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was a superior quality pig tail scroll. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from graphite. The rollers were made from masterfully worked iron by Cog Zatamerush. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled Factual Your Own Story, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It the method of writing a biography of oneself, particularly as it concerned military campaign or adventure. The writing was a bit stiff and it was somewhat self-indulgent. Overall, the prose was not awful, but not very good either.

In the late summer of 257, The Way With the Postulates was authored by her in Amudtun. It was stored in Mishthemrovol.

The Way With the Postulates was a legendary pig tail scroll. It was an exceptional pig tail scroll. It was made from pig tail cloth. The rollers were made from kaolinite. The rollers were made from exceptionally worked iron. Written on the scroll was a manual entitled The Way With the Postulates, authored by Zas Melbilthad. It concerned axiomatic reasoning. The writing was very rigid and it was quite self-indulgent. Overall, the prose was not awful, but not very good either.
